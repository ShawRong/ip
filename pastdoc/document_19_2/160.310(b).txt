# Law
160.310(b)
# Key Legal Concepts
Covered Entity: A covered entity is a healthcare provider, health plan, or healthcare clearinghouse that transmits any health information in electronic form in connection with a HIPAA transaction. Business Associate: A business associate refers to a person or entity that performs certain functions or activities on behalf of, or provides certain services to, a covered entity that involves the use or disclosure of protected health information.
# Key Legal Principles
Cooperation Requirement: Covered entities and business associates are legally required to cooperate with the Secretary of Health and Human Services during investigations and compliance reviews, ensuring transparency and accountability. Compliance with Administrative Simplification: Entities must adhere to the administrative simplification provisions of the law, which aim to improve the efficiency and effectiveness of the healthcare system.
# Application Scenarios
A healthcare provider receiving a request for information from the Secretary during a compliance review must provide all requested documentation and access to relevant personnel. A business associate involved in handling patient data must respond promptly to inquiries made by the Secretary regarding their data protection practices.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
This provision emphasizes the importance of accountability in the handling of protected health information, reinforcing the need for covered entities and business associates to adhere to regulations. By mandating cooperation with investigations, the law seeks to ensure that healthcare entities maintain compliance and protect patient information, which is central to the overall goals of HIPAA.
