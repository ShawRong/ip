# Law
160.314(b)(2)
# Key Legal Concepts
Investigational inquiries: These are non-public proceedings that focus on gathering information related to specific matters, initiated by the Secretary. Discretionary attendance: The Secretary has the authority to decide who may attend these proceedings, allowing for flexibility in managing participants.
# Key Legal Principles
Right to counsel: Witnesses have the inherent right to be accompanied and represented by an attorney during investigational inquiries, ensuring legal support and advice. Confidentiality: The non-public nature of these inquiries emphasizes the importance of confidentiality in the investigatory process.
# Application Scenarios
A business being investigated for alleged regulatory violations might attend an investigational inquiry where they can bring their legal counsel for guidance. The Secretary may choose to allow certain non-witness parties to attend an inquiry based on their relevance to the investigation.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
The provision reinforces the confidentiality and protective measures surrounding investigational inquiries, highlighting the balance between oversight and individual rights. It underscores the importance of legal representation in administrative proceedings, ensuring that witnesses can adequately defend their interests.
