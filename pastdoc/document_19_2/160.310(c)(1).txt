# Law
160.310(c)(1)
# Key Legal Concepts
**Covered Entity**: A covered entity refers to organizations such as healthcare providers, health plans, and healthcare clearinghouses that are subject to HIPAA regulations. **Business Associate**: A business associate is an individual or entity that performs certain functions on behalf of, or provides services to, a covered entity that involves the use or disclosure of protected health information (PHI). **Protected Health Information (PHI)**: PHI includes any individually identifiable health information held or transmitted by a covered entity or its business associates, relating to the health status, provision of healthcare, or payment for healthcare.
# Key Legal Principles
**Right of Access**: The law establishes the Secretary's right to access information necessary to ensure compliance with healthcare regulations, reinforcing the principle of transparency in health information management. **Exigent Circumstances**: The provision allows for immediate access by the Secretary without notice if there are concerns about the potential destruction or concealment of documents, emphasizing the importance of timely compliance checks.
# Application Scenarios
A healthcare provider may be audited by the Secretary to ensure compliance with HIPAA regulations, requiring the provider to grant access to their records and facilities during business hours. In a situation where there is suspicion of record tampering, the Secretary may demand access at any time to prevent loss of critical information.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
This law supports the overarching goal of enforcing healthcare regulations by ensuring that entities remain accountable for their handling of protected health information. It establishes a framework for oversight that protects patient privacy and ensures compliance with administrative simplification provisions, which is essential for maintaining trust in the healthcare system.
