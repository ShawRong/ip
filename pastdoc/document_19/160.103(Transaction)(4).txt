# Law
160.103(Transaction)(4)
# Key Legal Concepts
Transaction: Refers to the exchange of information between two parties to execute financial or administrative tasks in health care, which includes various forms of communication such as claims submissions and status inquiries. Health care claim status: This specific type of transaction pertains to the inquiry and reporting of the status of health care claims, providing updates on whether claims have been received, processed, or denied.
# Key Legal Principles
Confidentiality: The principle that information transmitted during health care transactions must be kept private and secure, protecting patient data from unauthorized access. Accountability: Ensures that both parties in a transaction are responsible for the accuracy and timeliness of the information exchanged, particularly in the context of claims and reimbursements.
# Application Scenarios
A health care provider submits a claim to an insurance company and later checks the claim status to confirm whether it has been processed. An insurance company uses a transaction to communicate the status of a claim back to the health care provider, indicating whether it was approved or denied.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
The inclusion of health care claim status as a type of transaction emphasizes the importance of efficient communication in the health care system, which is crucial for timely reimbursement and patient care. This law underscores the regulatory framework that governs the processes of information exchange between health care providers and insurers, aiming to enhance transparency and accountability in health care transactions.
