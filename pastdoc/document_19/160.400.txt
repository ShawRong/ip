# Key Legal Concepts
**Civil Money Penalty**: A financial penalty imposed by a governmental authority for violations of laws or regulations, intended to deter non-compliance and promote adherence to legal standards. **Secretary's Authority**: The power granted to the Secretary (of Health and Human Services) to impose civil money penalties under specific statutory provisions, ensuring enforcement of compliance within the healthcare sector.
# Key Legal Principles
**Due Process**: The principle that individuals and entities must be given fair notice and an opportunity to be heard before penalties can be imposed, ensuring that enforcement actions are conducted fairly. **Proportionality**: The principle that penalties should be proportionate to the severity and circumstances of the violation, preventing excessively harsh penalties for minor infractions.
# Application Scenarios
A healthcare provider failing to comply with patient privacy regulations may face a civil money penalty as determined by the Secretary. An entity that improperly discloses protected health information could be subjected to penalties under this subpart, reflecting the enforcement of compliance with healthcare laws.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
This law underscores the regulatory framework established to ensure adherence to health information privacy standards, reinforcing the government's role in enforcing compliance. The imposition of civil money penalties serves as a key mechanism for promoting accountability within the healthcare system, aligning with broader legislative goals of protecting patient rights.
