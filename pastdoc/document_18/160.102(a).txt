# Law
160.102(a)
# Key Legal Concepts
The term 'health plan' refers to any plan that provides health care benefits, which can include insurance companies, group health plans, and government programs like Medicare and Medicaid. The concept of a 'health care clearinghouse' involves entities that process or facilitate the processing of health information, acting as intermediaries between health care providers and insurers. A 'health care provider' is defined as any individual or organization that provides health care services and transmits health information electronically in relation to a covered transaction.
# Key Legal Principles
The principle of applicability indicates that the standards set forth in this subchapter are binding on the specified entities, meaning they must comply with established regulations. The requirement for electronic transmission of health information emphasizes the importance of digital compliance in health care transactions, enhancing efficiency and accuracy in data handling.
# Application Scenarios
A health care provider must ensure compliance with electronic billing standards when submitting claims to insurance companies. A health care clearinghouse must implement secure methods for handling patient data to comply with regulations.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
This law establishes a framework that defines which entities are subject to regulatory standards, thus laying the groundwork for compliance and accountability in health care transactions. By specifying the applicability of these regulations, the law aims to protect patient information and streamline health care operations across various stakeholders.
