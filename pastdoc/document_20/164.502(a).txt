# Law
164.502(a)
# Key Legal Concepts
Protected Health Information (PHI): This refers to any information about health status, provision of health care, or payment for health care that can be linked to an individual. Covered Entity: Organizations or individuals that are subject to HIPAA regulations, including healthcare providers, health plans, and healthcare clearinghouses that transmit any health information in electronic form. Business Associate: A person or entity that performs functions or activities on behalf of, or provides certain services to, a covered entity that involves the use or disclosure of protected health information.
# Key Legal Principles
Permitted Uses and Disclosures: Covered entities and business associates may use or disclose PHI only as permitted by law or required by their contracts. Required Disclosures: Covered entities must disclose PHI when required by law, ensuring compliance with legal mandates. Prohibited Uses and Disclosures: Any use or disclosure of PHI not expressly permitted or required by law is prohibited, safeguarding patient privacy.
# Application Scenarios
A healthcare provider sharing PHI with another provider for treatment purposes is a permitted disclosure. A business associate conducting billing services for a covered entity must adhere to the contract terms regarding the use of PHI.
