#### Step 1: Review of HIPAA Regulation IDs
The relevant regulation is **45 CFR 164.501**, which pertains to "Health care operations." Specifically, it outlines certain permissible disclosures of protected health information (PHI) without patient consent, including disclosures for legal purposes. Relevant sections include:

- **164.512(a)**: Permits disclosures when required by law.
- **164.512(e)**: Addresses disclosures to law enforcement officials in response to a subpoena.

These sections highlight that while patient consent is typically required for most disclosures, there are exceptions when compliance with legal processes is involved.

3. **Confidentiality and Ethics**: The hospital must still consider the ethical implications of disclosing sensitive mental health information. Although legally permissible, such disclosures should be handled carefully to maintain patient trust and confidentiality.

4. **Scope of Disclosure**: The hospital should limit the information disclosed to what is specifically requested in the subpoena, avoiding any unnecessary sharing of additional PHI.
