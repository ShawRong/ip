# Law
164.508(c)(1)(i)
# Key Legal Concepts
A valid authorization must describe the specific information that will be used or disclosed, ensuring clarity and understanding. The requirement for specificity in the description is intended to protect individuals' privacy by informing them exactly what data is involved.
# Key Legal Principles
The principle of informed consent: Individuals must fully understand what they are authorizing to ensure their consent is meaningful. The principle of data minimization: The authorization should limit the disclosure to only the necessary information, promoting privacy.
# Application Scenarios
A healthcare provider seeking to share patient records with a specialist must provide a detailed description of the specific records being shared. A company requesting employee consent to disclose personal data for marketing purposes must clearly outline what information will be used.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
This law emphasizes the importance of transparency in data handling, reinforcing the overall framework of protecting individuals' rights. It supports the overarching goal of ensuring that individuals maintain control over their personal information and understand the implications of its use.
