# Law
164.500(a)
# Key Legal Concepts
**Covered Entities**: Organizations or individuals who fall under the regulations, typically including healthcare providers, health plans, and healthcare clearinghouses that transmit any health information in electronic form. **Protected Health Information (PHI)**: Any information about health status, provision of healthcare, or payment for healthcare that can be linked to a specific individual, which must be safeguarded under the law.
# Key Legal Principles
**Applicability**: The law specifies that the standards and requirements are applicable to covered entities, setting clear boundaries for who must comply with the regulations. **Compliance**: Covered entities are required to implement specific standards and specifications to ensure the protection and confidentiality of PHI.
# Application Scenarios
Healthcare providers implementing electronic health records systems must adhere to the standards outlined in this law to protect patient information. Health insurance companies must ensure that their data handling practices meet the specified requirements to avoid breaches of PHI.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
This law establishes the foundational requirements for protecting PHI, which is critical to ensuring patient privacy and trust in healthcare systems. It serves as a vital component in a larger regulatory framework aimed at improving the security of health information in an increasingly digital landscape.
