# Law
164.510(b)(1)(ii)
# Key Legal Concepts
Protected Health Information (PHI): This refers to any health information that can be used to identify an individual and is maintained by a covered entity. Covered Entity: A term used to describe organizations that are subject to HIPAA regulations, including healthcare providers, health plans, and healthcare clearinghouses. Notification Purposes: The specific context in which PHI may be shared to inform family members or others responsible for the individual's care about their health status.
# Key Legal Principles
Professional Judgment: Covered entities must exercise professional judgment when determining the appropriateness of disclosing PHI, particularly in emergency situations or when the individual is incapacitated. Best Interests of the Individual: Disclosures should align with what is deemed to be in the best interests of the individual, considering their health care needs. Consent and Preferences: The principle that disclosures must respect any known preferences expressed by the individual regarding their health information.

# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
This law emphasizes the importance of communication and involvement of family members in the healthcare process while ensuring that individual privacy is respected. It establishes a framework that balances the need for information sharing with the protection of personal health information, thereby supporting coordinated care.
