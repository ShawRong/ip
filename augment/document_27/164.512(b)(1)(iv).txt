# Law
164.512(b)(1)(iv)
# Key Legal Concepts
Protected Health Information (PHI): Refers to any information about health status, provision of health care, or payment for health care that can be linked to an individual. Covered Entity: Individuals or organizations that are required to comply with HIPAA regulations, including health care providers, health plans, and health care clearinghouses. Public Health Activities: Actions performed by health authorities to protect and improve the health of populations, which may include disease prevention and control measures.
# Key Legal Principles
Permitted Disclosure: Covered entities are allowed to disclose PHI without individual consent for specific public health purposes, such as notifying individuals about exposure to communicable diseases. Legal Authorization: The disclosure to individuals at risk must be authorized by law, ensuring that public health interventions are conducted within legal frameworks. Risk Management: The principle of balancing individual privacy rights against the need for public health safety and intervention.
# Application Scenarios
Public health authorities notifying individuals who may have been exposed to a communicable disease during an outbreak. Health care providers disclosing pertinent health information to authorities for the purpose of tracking disease spread.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
This law emphasizes the importance of protecting public health while maintaining the privacy of individuals through regulated disclosures of PHI. It supports the overarching goals of public health law by allowing necessary interventions that may prevent the spread of communicable diseases.
