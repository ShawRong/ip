# Law
164.508(a)(2)(i)(C)
# Key Legal Concepts
**Covered Entity**: A covered entity refers to organizations or individuals that handle protected health information (PHI) and are required to comply with the HIPAA regulations. **Use or Disclosure of PHI**: This concept pertains to how a covered entity can utilize or share an individual's health information under specific conditions, particularly in legal contexts.
# Key Legal Principles
**Right to Defend**: This principle allows a covered entity to use or disclose PHI to defend itself in legal actions, emphasizing the balance between individual privacy rights and the entity's need to protect its legal interests. **Minimum Necessary Standard**: When disclosing information, covered entities must adhere to the principle of using the minimum necessary information to achieve the purpose of the disclosure.
# Application Scenarios
A health care provider facing a malpractice lawsuit might disclose relevant patient information to their legal counsel to prepare a defense. A health insurance company may release certain claims data in response to a lawsuit from a policyholder regarding denied claims.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
This provision underscores the legal rights of covered entities to protect themselves while navigating the complexities of HIPAA, which primarily aims to safeguard patient privacy. It also highlights the tension between individual rights and institutional defenses, indicating the necessity for clear legal frameworks governing health information use.
