# Law
164.504(e)(4)
# Key Legal Concepts
Business Associate: A person or entity that performs functions or activities on behalf of, or provides certain services to, a covered entity that involves the use or disclosure of protected health information (PHI). Protected Health Information (PHI): Any information about health status, provision of health care, or payment for health care that can be linked to an individual. Contractual Permissions: The specific rights and obligations outlined in agreements between covered entities and business associates regarding the use and disclosure of PHI.
# Key Legal Principles
Privacy and Security: Ensuring that PHI is used and disclosed only in accordance with the terms of contracts and applicable laws to protect patient privacy. Liability and Accountability: Business associates are held accountable for safeguarding PHI and may be liable for breaches of this information. Subcontractor Obligations: Business associates must ensure that any subcontractors comply with the same requirements regarding PHI as they do themselves.
# Application Scenarios
A healthcare provider contracts with a billing company (business associate) to manage patient billing while ensuring that patient information is handled according to HIPAA regulations. A health insurance company enters into an agreement with a data analytics firm (another business associate) to analyze claims data while ensuring that PHI is disclosed only as permitted by the original contract.
