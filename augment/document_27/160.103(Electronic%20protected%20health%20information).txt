# Law
160.103(Electronic%20protected%20health%20information)
# Key Legal Concepts
Electronic protected health information (ePHI) refers to any information that is created, stored, transmitted, or received in electronic form that relates to an individual's health status, provision of health care, or payment for health care. The definitions provided in the law establish the framework for understanding what constitutes ePHI, thus ensuring clarity in legal and regulatory contexts.
# Key Legal Principles
Confidentiality: The principle that ePHI must be kept secure and private, protecting individuals' sensitive health information from unauthorized access. Compliance: Entities handling ePHI are required to adhere to legal standards and regulations governing its management, ensuring accountability and protection of patient rights.
# Application Scenarios
Healthcare providers using electronic health records (EHRs) must ensure that they are compliant with regulations concerning ePHI to protect patient privacy. Health insurance companies must implement safeguards for ePHI during electronic transactions, such as claims processing, to prevent data breaches.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
The definitions in this section are foundational for understanding how ePHI is treated under the law, guiding the implementation of privacy and security measures. This law supports larger legal frameworks by clarifying what information is protected, thus reinforcing the importance of safeguarding sensitive health data in a digital age.
