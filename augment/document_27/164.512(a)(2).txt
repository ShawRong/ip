# Law
164.512(a)(2)
# Key Legal Concepts
Covered Entity: An entity that is subject to HIPAA regulations, which includes healthcare providers, health plans, and healthcare clearinghouses that transmit health information electronically. Protected Health Information (PHI): Any individually identifiable health information that is transmitted or maintained in any form, which is subject to privacy protections under HIPAA. Disclosures Required by Law: Situations where a covered entity is mandated to disclose PHI in compliance with specific legal requirements.
# Key Legal Principles
Compliance with Legal Requirements: Covered entities must adhere to various legal standards when disclosing PHI, ensuring that disclosures are permissible under the law. Protection of Privacy: While disclosures may be legally required, the law emphasizes the protection of individuals' privacy, ensuring that any disclosure is limited to necessary information. Conditional Disclosure: The law stipulates that disclosures for law enforcement, judicial proceedings, and victim-related situations must meet certain conditions to protect individuals' rights.
# Application Scenarios
A healthcare provider must disclose patient information to comply with a subpoena in a legal case, ensuring that the disclosure meets the conditions outlined in the relevant paragraphs. A hospital may report suspected abuse to law enforcement as required by state law, adhering to the specified guidelines in paragraph (f) to protect the interests of the victim.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
The provision emphasizes the balance between legal obligations and the protection of patient privacy rights, reinforcing the importance of compliance with both HIPAA and other legal requirements. It highlights the necessity for covered entities to navigate complex legal frameworks while ensuring that any disclosures are justified and appropriately limited to safeguard individuals' health information.
