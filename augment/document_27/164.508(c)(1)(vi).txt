# Law
164.508(c)(1)(vi)
# Key Legal Concepts
Authorization: A formal permission granted by an individual allowing another party to use their personal information in specific ways. Personal Representative: An individual authorized to act on behalf of another person, particularly in legal or medical contexts.
# Key Legal Principles
Informed Consent: The individual must fully understand what they are authorizing, including the scope and purpose of the information usage. Accountability: The authorization must clearly document who is acting on behalf of the individual and the extent of their authority.
# Application Scenarios
A healthcare provider requires authorization from a patient for sharing medical records with a third party. A legal guardian must provide authorization for a minor's medical treatment, including a description of their authority.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
This law emphasizes the need for clear documentation in the authorization process, reinforcing the principles of consent and accountability. It ensures that individuals' rights are protected by requiring detailed information about who can act on their behalf and under what circumstances.
