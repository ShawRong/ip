# Law
164.504(e)(4)(ii)(B)(2)
# Key Legal Concepts
Confidentiality: This concept refers to the obligation to protect sensitive information from unauthorized access and disclosure. Business Associate: A business associate is a person or entity that performs certain functions or activities on behalf of, or provides certain services to, a covered entity that involves the use or disclosure of protected health information (PHI).
# Key Legal Principles
Notification Obligations: The law establishes a duty for individuals to inform business associates of any known breaches of confidentiality regarding sensitive information. Accountability: This principle emphasizes that parties involved in handling confidential information must take responsibility for safeguarding that information and promptly reporting any breaches.
# Application Scenarios
A healthcare provider discovers that patient data has been exposed due to a cyber incident and must notify its business associate to address the breach. A company that handles sensitive financial information learns of a data leak and is required to inform its service provider to mitigate potential risks.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
This law reinforces the importance of maintaining the confidentiality of sensitive information and the responsibility of individuals to report breaches, thereby enhancing overall data protection. It supports the broader legal framework that governs the handling of protected information, ensuring that all parties involved are vigilant and proactive in safeguarding confidential data.
