# Law
160.404(b)(2)(iii)(A)
# Key Legal Concepts
Willful Neglect: This refers to a conscious, intentional failure to act or a reckless disregard for the known consequences of one's actions regarding compliance with legal standards. Correction Period: This concept highlights the importance of a 30-day period during which a covered entity or business associate can rectify a violation without facing the maximum penalties.
# Key Legal Principles
Proportional Penalties: The law establishes a range of fines for violations based on the severity and context of the infraction, ensuring that penalties are appropriate relative to the violation's nature. Reasonable Diligence: The expectation that covered entities or business associates should actively monitor compliance and be aware of potential violations, which affects liability and penalty assessment.
# Application Scenarios
A healthcare provider discovers a data breach that falls under willful neglect. If they correct the issue within 30 days, they may avoid the higher penalty range. A business associate realizes that they have failed to implement a required safeguard but takes prompt action to rectify the situation within the designated timeframe.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
This provision emphasizes the importance of proactive compliance and encourages entities to address violations swiftly to mitigate penalties. The focus on willful neglect and the ability to correct violations within a specific timeframe underscores a framework that seeks to balance accountability with the opportunity for remediation.
