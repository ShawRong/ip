# Law
160.314(a)(1)(iii)
# Key Legal Concepts
Subpoena: A legal document that orders an individual to appear in court or produce evidence. Testimony: A formal statement or declaration made under oath, often used as evidence in legal proceedings.
# Key Legal Principles
Due Process: The principle that legal proceedings must be conducted fairly and with adequate notice to the parties involved. Right to Counsel: The principle that individuals have the right to legal representation during legal proceedings, including when responding to a subpoena.
# Application Scenarios
A party in a lawsuit receives a subpoena requiring them to testify about specific events related to the case. A witness is subpoenaed to provide testimony at a scheduled time and place, ensuring they are aware of the legal obligations.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
This law emphasizes the importance of clear communication regarding the logistics of testimony, ensuring all parties understand when and where they must appear. It reinforces the need for proper procedural adherence in legal processes, contributing to the integrity of the judicial system.
