# Law
160.314(b)(1)
# Key Legal Concepts
Investigational inquiries: These are non-public proceedings initiated by the Secretary to gather information, likely related to regulatory or compliance matters. Testimony under oath: This requires individuals to provide truthful statements during the investigational inquiry, emphasizing the importance of honesty in legal proceedings.
# Key Legal Principles
Due process: Individuals involved in investigational inquiries have the right to present their testimony under oath, ensuring fair treatment during the investigative process. Confidentiality: The non-public nature of these inquiries protects sensitive information and the parties involved from public scrutiny.
# Application Scenarios
An individual being questioned about compliance with regulatory standards during a private investigational inquiry. A company providing testimony regarding its business practices in response to a non-public investigation by the Secretary.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
The requirement for testimony under oath reinforces the seriousness and integrity of the investigational process, supporting the law's goal of thorough and fair inquiry. The non-public nature of these proceedings, combined with sworn testimony, helps to maintain confidentiality while ensuring that truthful information is elicited.
