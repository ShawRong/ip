# Law
160.300
# Key Legal Concepts
**Covered Entities**: These are organizations or individuals who must comply with specific regulations under this law, including healthcare providers, health plans, and healthcare clearinghouses. **Business Associates**: Refers to individuals or entities that perform certain functions or activities on behalf of, or provide certain services to, a covered entity that involves the use or disclosure of protected health information (PHI). **Compliance**: This concept relates to the obligation of covered entities and business associates to adhere to the standards and provisions set forth in this law and related regulations.
# Key Legal Principles
**Enforcement Authority**: The Secretary has the power to enforce compliance with the privacy and security regulations, ensuring that covered entities and business associates adhere to the law. **Accountability**: Covered entities and business associates are held accountable for their compliance with the law, emphasizing the importance of proper management of PHI. **Transparency**: This principle underlines the need for clear processes and documentation to demonstrate compliance efforts by covered entities and business associates.
# Application Scenarios
**Audits and Inspections**: The Secretary may conduct audits to verify that covered entities are complying with the regulations. Failure to comply may lead to penalties. **Investigations of Breaches**: In the event of a data breach involving PHI, the Secretary will investigate to determine if the covered entity or business associate was compliant with the law.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
**Framework for Compliance**: This section establishes the foundation for ensuring that covered entities and business associates understand their responsibilities under the law, thus supporting the overall purpose of protecting patient information. **Support for Regulatory Enforcement**: By outlining the applicability of the law, it reinforces the authority of the Secretary to enforce compliance, which is crucial for maintaining the integrity of health information privacy.
