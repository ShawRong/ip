# Law
160.103(Genetic%20information)(1)
# Key Legal Concepts
**Genetic Information**: This refers to data regarding an individual's genetic tests, tests of family members, the manifestation of diseases in family members, and any requests or participation in genetic services or research. **Individual and Family Context**: The definition extends to not only the individual but also their family members, recognizing the interconnectedness of genetic information.
# Key Legal Principles
**Privacy and Confidentiality**: The law emphasizes the importance of protecting genetic information as sensitive personal data, which requires careful handling to maintain individual privacy. **Informed Consent**: Participation in genetic testing and services necessitates informed consent, ensuring that individuals are aware of how their genetic data will be used.
# Application Scenarios
A healthcare provider conducting genetic tests must ensure that the genetic information is kept confidential and shared only with authorized personnel. A researcher conducting a study on genetic predispositions must obtain informed consent from participants regarding the use of their genetic data.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
This law forms a foundational aspect of genetic privacy rights, highlighting the need for protections specific to genetic information. It supports broader legal frameworks aimed at safeguarding personal data in the context of advancements in genetic research and testing.
