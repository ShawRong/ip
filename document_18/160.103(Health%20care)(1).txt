# Law
160.103(Health%20care)(1)
# Key Legal Concepts
Health care encompasses a broad range of services aimed at maintaining or improving an individual's health. This includes preventive, diagnostic, therapeutic, rehabilitative, maintenance, and palliative care. Counseling and assessment are integral parts of health care, addressing both physical and mental health needs, and ensuring comprehensive treatment approaches.
# Key Legal Principles
The principle of comprehensive care implies that health services should address all aspects of an individual's well-being, not just specific ailments. The principle of patient-centered care emphasizes the importance of tailoring health services to the unique needs and conditions of each individual.
# Application Scenarios
A patient receiving preventive care, such as vaccinations or regular health screenings, to avoid potential health issues. A mental health professional providing counseling services to address a patient's psychological well-being as part of their overall health care plan.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
This law supports the argument that health care is a multifaceted concept, encompassing various types of care and interventions. It reinforces the idea that health care should be holistic, addressing both physical and mental health to improve overall health outcomes.
