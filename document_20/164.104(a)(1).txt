# Law
164.104(a)(1)
# Key Legal Concepts
A health plan is defined as any entity that provides, or pays for the cost of, health care services, including insurance companies and managed care organizations. The term 'standards, requirements, and implementation specifications' refers to the specific guidelines that health plans must follow to ensure compliance with privacy and security regulations.
# Key Legal Principles
The principle of compliance mandates that health plans must adhere to established standards to protect patient information and ensure the confidentiality of health records. The principle of applicability indicates that the regulations apply uniformly to all health plans unless explicitly exempted by other provisions.
# Application Scenarios
A health plan implementing new privacy protocols in response to updated regulations under this law. A health plan undergoing an audit to verify compliance with the specified standards and requirements.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
This law establishes the foundational compliance requirements for health plans, emphasizing their role in safeguarding patient information. By outlining the applicability of standards to health plans, the law supports broader goals of enhancing patient privacy and security in health care.
