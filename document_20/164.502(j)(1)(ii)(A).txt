# Law
164.502(j)(1)(ii)(A)
# Key Legal Concepts
Health oversight agencies: These are governmental or authorized entities responsible for monitoring and regulating health care practices to ensure compliance with laws and standards. Public health authorities: These organizations focus on protecting and improving the health of populations through organized efforts and informed choices. Covered entity: This refers to organizations that handle protected health information (PHI) under HIPAA, such as health care providers and insurers.
# Key Legal Principles
Confidentiality: The principle that health information must be kept private and disclosed only under specific, legally authorized circumstances. Accountability: Ensures that health care providers adhere to professional standards and can be held responsible for misconduct. Compliance with regulations: Covered entities must follow laws governing health information disclosure, particularly those related to oversight and public health.
# Application Scenarios
Reporting potential misconduct: If a health care provider is suspected of not meeting professional standards, the information may be disclosed to a health oversight agency for investigation. Accreditation processes: Health care organizations might disclose information to accreditation bodies to demonstrate compliance with established health care standards.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
Strengthening oversight: This law supports the broader argument of enhancing oversight and accountability in health care by allowing disclosures that facilitate investigations. Protecting public health: By permitting disclosures to health authorities, the law reinforces the commitment to safeguarding public health through rigorous oversight of health care entities.
