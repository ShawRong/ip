# Law
160.103(Health%20information)(1)
# Key Legal Concepts
Health information encompasses a broad range of data, including both verbal and written forms, covering personal health records, medical histories, and genetic data. Entities such as health care providers, health plans, and public health authorities are recognized as key stakeholders in the creation and handling of health information.
# Key Legal Principles
Confidentiality of health information is paramount, emphasizing the need for privacy protections for sensitive personal data. Health information must be securely managed to ensure that it is only accessed or disclosed by authorized individuals or entities.
# Application Scenarios
A health care provider collecting patient medical histories must ensure that the information is stored securely and shared only with relevant parties. A school or university conducting health screenings must handle student health information in compliance with privacy regulations.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
This law supports the overarching aim of protecting individual privacy rights in health care by defining what constitutes health information and who is responsible for its management. The identification of various stakeholders involved in health information handling reinforces the need for comprehensive policies to safeguard sensitive data.
