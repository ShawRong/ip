# Law
160.103(Protected%20health%20information)(1)(ii)
# Key Legal Concepts
Protected Health Information (PHI): This refers to any information about health status, provision of health care, or health care payment that can be linked to an individual. It is crucial for safeguarding patient privacy. Electronic Media: This concept pertains to any digital storage or transmission methods of PHI, which can include computers, servers, and online systems.
# Key Legal Principles
Confidentiality: The principle that PHI must be kept private and only accessible to authorized individuals to protect patient privacy. Security: This principle emphasizes the need for appropriate safeguards to protect PHI maintained in electronic formats from unauthorized access and breaches.
# Application Scenarios
A healthcare provider using an electronic health record (EHR) system must ensure that all patient information stored is secure and only accessible to authorized staff. Telehealth services must comply with regulations regarding the transmission and storage of PHI to protect patient confidentiality.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
This law emphasizes the critical nature of electronic storage and transmission of PHI, reinforcing the need for enhanced security measures. It supports the broader framework of health privacy laws by specifying the conditions under which PHI must be protected, particularly in electronic formats.
