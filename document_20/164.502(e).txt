# Law
164.502(e)
# Key Legal Concepts
**Protected Health Information (PHI)**: Refers to any individually identifiable health information that is held by a covered entity or its business associates. **Covered Entity**: This includes health care providers, health plans, and health care clearinghouses that transmit any health information in electronic form in connection with a HIPAA transaction. **Business Associate**: Refers to a person or entity that performs certain functions or activities on behalf of, or provides certain services to, a covered entity that involves the use or disclosure of protected health information.
# Key Legal Principles
**Disclosure Standards**: Covered entities must have a written contract with business associates that outlines how PHI will be handled, ensuring compliance with HIPAA regulations. **Compliance Obligations**: Both covered entities and business associates have a duty to monitor compliance and rectify any breaches of contract or violations of privacy obligations. **Material Breach Notification**: If either party becomes aware of a material breach or violation, they must take reasonable steps to address it, which may include terminating the agreement if necessary.
# Application Scenarios
A health care provider hires a billing company (business associate) to manage patient billing, necessitating a contract that specifies how PHI will be protected. A health insurance company discovers that its third-party claims processor has mishandled PHI and must take steps to rectify the situation, potentially leading to contract termination.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
These principles establish a framework for protecting patient health information while allowing for necessary disclosures to business associates. Ensuring compliance with these standards reinforces the legal obligations of both covered entities and business associates, thereby enhancing the overall integrity of health information privacy.
