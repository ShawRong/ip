# Law
160.420(a)(5)
# Key Legal Concepts
**Civil Money Penalty**: A financial penalty imposed by a government authority for violations of regulations or laws, aimed at deterring future non-compliance. **Mitigating and Aggravating Factors**: Factors that can lessen (mitigate) or increase (aggravate) the severity of a penalty, considered in the assessment of the appropriate amount.
# Key Legal Principles
**Due Process**: The requirement that a respondent be given fair notice of a proposed penalty, allowing them the opportunity to respond or contest the determination. **Proportionality**: The principle that the penalty should be proportionate to the nature and severity of the violation, reflecting the specific circumstances of each case.
# Application Scenarios
A healthcare provider receiving a proposed civil money penalty must be informed of the specific factors considered that justify the penalty amount. An organization facing penalties can present evidence of mitigating factors to potentially reduce the severity of the sanctions imposed.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
The law emphasizes transparency in the penalty assessment process by requiring the Secretary to communicate the factors influencing the penalty amount. It reinforces the idea that penalties should be tailored to the specifics of each case, ensuring fairness and accountability in regulatory enforcement.
