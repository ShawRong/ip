# Law
160.103(Health%20plan)
# Key Legal Concepts
**Health Plan Definition**: A health plan is defined as an individual or group plan that provides or pays for medical care, indicating the framework under which health insurance operates. **Medical Care**: The term 'medical care' is further defined in section 2791(a)(2) of the Public Health Service Act (PHS Act), which clarifies what constitutes medical care within the scope of health plans.
# Key Legal Principles
**Inclusion and Exclusion Criteria**: The definitions section outlines what is included and excluded under the term 'health plan', which is crucial for determining the applicability of the law to various health insurance products. **Regulatory Compliance**: The necessity for plans to align with federal definitions ensures compliance with broader healthcare regulations, promoting standardized understanding across different jurisdictions.
# Application Scenarios
A health insurance provider must determine if their product qualifies as a health plan according to the specified definition for regulatory purposes. A consumer evaluating their health insurance options needs to understand whether a particular plan meets the legal definition to ensure it provides adequate coverage.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
The definitions provided are foundational for interpreting subsequent provisions of the law, clarifying the scope of coverage and the rights of both providers and consumers. Understanding these definitions helps stakeholders navigate compliance and enforcement issues within the healthcare system, supporting the overarching goals of the subchapter.
