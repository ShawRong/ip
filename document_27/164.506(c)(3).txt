# Law
164.506(c)(3)
# Key Legal Concepts
Protected Health Information (PHI): Refers to any individually identifiable health information that is held or transmitted by a covered entity, which is protected under HIPAA. Covered Entity: An organization or individual that provides healthcare services and transmits health information electronically, thereby being subject to HIPAA regulations.
# Key Legal Principles
Minimum Necessary Standard: Covered entities must limit the disclosure of PHI to the minimum necessary to achieve the intended purpose of the disclosure. Permitted Disclosure: Under HIPAA, covered entities are allowed to disclose PHI to another covered entity for payment activities, emphasizing the importance of sharing information for billing and insurance purposes.
# Application Scenarios
A hospital disclosing patient billing information to an insurance company for the purpose of processing a claim. A healthcare provider sharing a patient's treatment details with another provider for the purpose of coordinating payment for services rendered.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
This law supports the facilitation of efficient payment processes within healthcare, ensuring that providers can receive compensation for services while maintaining patient confidentiality. It reinforces the legal framework that governs the sharing of PHI, balancing the need for information exchange in healthcare operations with the imperative of protecting patient privacy.
