# Law
164.502(b)(2)(iv)
# Key Legal Concepts
Minimum Necessary: This concept refers to the principle that covered entities must limit the use and disclosure of protected health information (PHI) to the minimum necessary to accomplish the intended purpose. Disclosures to the Secretary: This refers to the specific context in which health information can be disclosed without applying the minimum necessary standard, particularly when disclosures are made to the Secretary of Health and Human Services.
# Key Legal Principles
Compliance with Regulations: The principle emphasizes the obligation of covered entities to comply with specific regulations regarding the handling and disclosure of PHI, particularly under HIPAA. Public Health and Oversight: This principle underscores the importance of allowing certain disclosures to government officials to facilitate oversight and compliance with health regulations.
# Application Scenarios
Reporting to the Secretary for compliance audits or investigations where PHI might be disclosed without adhering to the minimum necessary standard. Submitting required documentation or reports to the Secretary that may include PHI necessary for regulatory purposes.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
The provision highlights a clear exception within the broader HIPAA regulations, showcasing the balance between protecting individual privacy and allowing for necessary governmental oversight. It reinforces the importance of regulatory compliance by illustrating scenarios where the minimum necessary standard does not apply, thereby ensuring that oversight functions can be performed effectively.
