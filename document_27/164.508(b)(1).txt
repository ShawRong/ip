# Law
164.508(b)(1)
# Key Legal Concepts
A valid authorization is a formal document that complies with specific requirements set out in the law, ensuring that the consent given by an individual is both informed and legally binding. The inclusion of additional elements or information in a valid authorization is permissible as long as they do not conflict with the core requirements, allowing for flexibility while maintaining legal integrity.
# Key Legal Principles
The principle of informed consent, which requires that individuals understand what they are consenting to and the implications of their authorization. The principle of specificity, which mandates that authorizations must clearly outline what is being authorized and under what conditions, fostering transparency in the consent process.
# Application Scenarios
A healthcare provider seeking to share a patient's medical information with a third party must obtain a valid authorization that meets the outlined specifications to ensure compliance with legal standards. A researcher conducting a study involving personal health information must secure valid authorizations from participants, detailing the use of their data while adhering to the legal requirements.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
This law emphasizes the importance of valid authorizations in protecting individual privacy rights while allowing for the necessary sharing of information in specific contexts. It serves as a foundation for ensuring that all parties involved in the authorization process are aware of their rights and obligations, ultimately supporting ethical practices in information handling.
