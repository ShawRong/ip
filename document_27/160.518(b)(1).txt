# Law
160.518(b)(1)
# Key Legal Concepts
Evidence Admission: This concept pertains to the rules governing what evidence can be presented in a hearing, emphasizing the importance of pre-hearing disclosure. Exclusion of Evidence: This principle deals with the circumstances under which evidence can be excluded from consideration based on procedural failures, such as not providing evidence as required.
# Key Legal Principles
Due Process: The requirement for parties to exchange evidence ensures fairness in legal proceedings, allowing both sides to prepare adequately. Judicial Discretion: The Administrative Law Judge (ALJ) has the authority to decide whether to exclude evidence based on compliance with procedural rules.
# Application Scenarios
A party may object to the admission of a document that was not exchanged within the specified timeframe, prompting an ALJ review. If a party fails to provide a witness statement as required, the opposing party can raise an objection during the hearing.
