# Law
164.501Dataaggregation
# Key Legal Concepts
Protected Health Information (PHI): Refers to any information related to health status, provision of health care, or payment for health care that can be linked to an individual. Business Associate: An entity that performs functions or activities on behalf of a covered entity that involves the use or disclosure of PHI. Data Aggregation: The process of combining PHI from different covered entities to conduct analyses related to the health care operations of those entities.
# Key Legal Principles
Confidentiality: The principle that PHI must be kept secure and disclosed only in accordance with legal regulations. Compliance: Business associates must comply with HIPAA regulations when handling PHI, ensuring it is used only for permissible purposes. Permitted Uses and Disclosures: Business associates can aggregate data only for purposes that align with the operations of the covered entities they serve.
# Application Scenarios
A healthcare analytics company performing data analysis for multiple hospitals to identify trends in patient care and outcomes. An insurance company aggregating claims data from various health providers to better evaluate the effectiveness of treatments.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
This law clarifies the definitions and scope of data aggregation in relation to PHI, which is crucial for understanding compliance obligations. It underscores the importance of safeguarding patient information while allowing for beneficial data analysis among covered entities.
