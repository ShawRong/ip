# Law
164.510(b)(5)
# Key Legal Concepts
Protected Health Information (PHI): This refers to any information about health status, provision of health care, or payment for health care that can be linked to an individual. Covered Entity: Entities that are subject to HIPAA regulations, including healthcare providers, health plans, and healthcare clearinghouses. Disclosure: The act of releasing, transferring, or providing access to PHI.
# Key Legal Principles
Informed Consent: The principle that individuals have the right to control who accesses their health information, including after their death. Family Involvement: The law allows disclosures to family members or others involved in the individual's care, emphasizing the importance of family in healthcare contexts. Respect for Preferences: The requirement that disclosures must respect any known preferences expressed by the individual regarding their health information.
# Application Scenarios
A family member inquires about the health status of a deceased individual who was previously involved in their care and payment for healthcare services. # Relationship to Overall Document Argument
This provision reinforces the importance of family involvement in healthcare decisions, even after an individual's death, ensuring continuity of care and support. It aligns with the overarching goals of HIPAA to protect patient privacy while allowing necessary communication about health matters to those who were involved in the individual's care.
