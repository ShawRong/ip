# Law
160.103(Individually%20identifiable%20health%20information)(2)(i)
# Key Legal Concepts
Individually identifiable health information: This refers to any health information that can be linked to a specific individual, ensuring that the individual's identity can be discerned from the data. Health care provision: This encompasses all services related to the diagnosis, treatment, and care of an individual's physical or mental health, which can include medical records, treatment history, and payment details.
# Key Legal Principles
Confidentiality: The principle emphasizes protecting the privacy of individuals by ensuring that their identifiable health information is not disclosed without consent. Data protection: This principle involves safeguarding personal health information from unauthorized access and usage, aligning with regulations such as HIPAA.
# Application Scenarios
Medical records management: Healthcare providers must ensure that any records containing individually identifiable health information are stored securely and accessed only by authorized personnel. Insurance claims processing: When insurers process claims, they must handle any health information in a way that complies with privacy regulations to prevent unauthorized disclosure.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
This law helps establish the framework for what constitutes individually identifiable health information, which is essential for understanding privacy protections under health care laws. By defining the identification of individuals through health information, it underscores the importance of consent and confidentiality in the healthcare sector.
