# Law
160.408(e)
# Key Legal Concepts
**Civil Money Penalty**: A financial penalty imposed by a regulatory authority for violations of laws or regulations. This penalty is intended to deter future violations and enforce compliance. **Mitigating and Aggravating Factors**: Circumstances that can lessen (mitigate) or increase (aggravate) the severity of the penalty. These factors are assessed to ensure a fair and proportional response to the violation.
# Key Legal Principles
**Proportionality**: The principle that penalties should be proportionate to the severity of the violation and the circumstances surrounding it. **Discretion in Penalty Assessment**: The Secretary has the discretion to consider various factors, including those that may not be explicitly listed, to ensure that the penalty reflects the specifics of each case.
# Application Scenarios
Assessment of penalties in cases of non-compliance with healthcare regulations, where different factors such as intent, harm caused, and cooperation with investigations are considered. Situations involving repeated violations, where the Secretary might weigh previous infractions more heavily to determine an appropriate penalty.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
The inclusion of 'such other matters as justice may require' emphasizes the need for flexibility in penalty assessments, allowing for a tailored approach that considers the unique aspects of each case. This provision supports the overarching goal of achieving fair and just outcomes in regulatory enforcement, ensuring that penalties serve both punitive and corrective purposes.
