# Law
160.528(a)
# Key Legal Concepts
**Motion**: A formal request made to a judge or an administrative law judge (ALJ) for a specific ruling or order. **Relief Sought**: The specific outcome or remedy that the party making the motion is requesting from the ALJ.
# Key Legal Principles
**Due Process**: All parties must be properly notified of motions filed, allowing them an opportunity to respond, ensuring fairness in the legal process. **Clarity and Specificity**: Motions must be clear and specific regarding the relief sought and the legal authority being cited, which helps in efficient adjudication.
# Application Scenarios
Filing a motion for summary judgment in an administrative hearing where a party believes there are no material facts in dispute. Requesting a ruling on the admissibility of certain evidence in an administrative proceeding.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
The requirement for motions to be filed with the ALJ emphasizes the structured procedural framework within which administrative hearings operate. The need for clear articulation of the relief sought reinforces the importance of precise legal arguments, contributing to the integrity of the administrative process.
