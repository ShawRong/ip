# Law
164.510(b)(2)(i)
# Key Legal Concepts
Protected Health Information (PHI): This refers to any information about health status, provision of health care, or payment for health care that can be linked to an individual, which is subject to privacy regulations. Covered Entity: This term refers to health care providers, health plans, and health care clearinghouses that transmit any health information in electronic form during a transaction. Individual's Capacity: This concept pertains to the ability of an individual to make informed decisions regarding their health care, which can impact their rights to control the use of their health information.
# Key Legal Principles
Consent Requirement: The principle that a covered entity must obtain the individual's agreement before disclosing their protected health information, ensuring respect for patient autonomy. Presence and Availability: This principle emphasizes the importance of the individual's presence or availability in the context of sharing health information, reinforcing the need for direct consent.
# Application Scenarios
A healthcare provider discussing treatment options with a patient in a consultation room, where the patient can agree to share their health information with other providers. A hospital seeking consent from a patient before sharing their medical history with a specialist, ensuring the patient is present and can make decisions about their information.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
This law emphasizes the importance of patient consent as a fundamental aspect of health information privacy, aligning with broader regulatory goals of safeguarding individual rights. It supports the overall framework of health information privacy by ensuring that disclosures occur only with the individual's agreement, fostering trust between patients and healthcare providers.
