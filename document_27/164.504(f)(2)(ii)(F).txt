# Key Legal Concepts
**Protected Health Information (PHI)**: Refers to any information that can be used to identify an individual and relates to their health status, healthcare provision, or payment for healthcare. Laws ensure that PHI is handled with confidentiality and integrity. **Plan Sponsor**: This is an entity that establishes or maintains an employee benefit plan. The plan sponsor must comply with specific regulations regarding the handling of PHI, particularly in relation to amendments and disclosures.
# Key Legal Principles
**Amendment Rights**: Individuals have the right to request amendments to their protected health information. This principle ensures that the information used in healthcare decisions is accurate and up-to-date. **Certification Requirement**: The law mandates that a plan sponsor must certify that plan documents have been amended to include specific provisions before any PHI can be disclosed. This principle emphasizes accountability and compliance in the management of PHI.
