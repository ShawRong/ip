# Law
164.508(a)(2)(ii)
# Key Legal Concepts
**Authorization for Psychotherapy Notes**: This concept emphasizes that a covered entity must obtain explicit authorization from the patient for any use or disclosure of psychotherapy notes, except in specific circumstances outlined by the law. **Health Oversight Activities**: This principle allows for the disclosure of protected health information for the purpose of health oversight, ensuring that entities comply with regulatory standards. **Permitted Disclosures**: The law outlines specific scenarios where disclosures of protected health information are permitted without patient authorization, such as for legal requirements or health oversight activities.
# Key Legal Principles
**Compliance with Regulations**: Covered entities must adhere to regulations that govern the use and disclosure of protected health information, particularly concerning psychotherapy notes and other sensitive health data. **Protected Health Information (PHI)**: The principle of maintaining the confidentiality of PHI is critical, allowing disclosures only under defined legal circumstances to protect patient privacy.
