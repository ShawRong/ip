# Law
160.103(Genetic%20information)(1)(ii)
# Key Legal Concepts
Genetic information: This refers to data related to the genetic characteristics of an individual, which can include inherited traits and the results of genetic tests. Family member's genetic tests: This concept highlights that the genetic information of an individual's relatives is also included, emphasizing the interconnectedness of genetic data within families.
# Key Legal Principles
Confidentiality of genetic information: The law underscores the importance of protecting sensitive genetic information from unauthorized access and disclosure. Non-discrimination: Legal protections often exist to ensure that individuals are not discriminated against based on their genetic information or that of their family members.
# Application Scenarios
A healthcare provider must obtain informed consent before conducting genetic tests on a family member, ensuring that the individual understands how their genetic information may be used. An employer cannot make employment decisions based on the genetic test results of an employee's family member, thereby protecting against discrimination.
# Relationship to Overall Document Argument
This law strengthens the argument for the protection of genetic information by acknowledging the importance of family connections in understanding genetic traits. It reinforces the need for comprehensive privacy measures that protect not only individual genetic data but also that of family members, thereby supporting broader public health and ethical considerations.
